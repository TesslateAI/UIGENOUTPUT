About Dr. Rostova

Dr. Eva Rostova is a leading researcher in Cognitive Linguistics and Computational Semantics, with a particular focus on the intersection of language, thought, and artificial intelligence. Her work explores how language structures human cognition and how these insights can be applied to develop more intuitive and effective natural language processing systems.

With a Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge and postdoctoral experience at MIT, Dr. Rostova has published extensively in top-tier journals and presented at international conferences. She is committed to interdisciplinary collaboration and fostering the next generation of linguistic researchers.

Current Research Focus

Cognitive Linguistics & Conceptual Metaphor

Investigating how abstract concepts are structured and understood through metaphorical mappings in natural language. This includes exploring how these mappings influence reasoning and problem-solving.

Learn More

Computational Semantics & NLP

Developing algorithms and models for natural language understanding, particularly focusing on semantic representation and knowledge extraction from unstructured text. Aims to bridge linguistic theory with practical AI applications.

Learn More

Language & Cognition in AI

Exploring the cognitive foundations of language to inform the design of more human-like AI systems. This includes studying how humans process language and how these processes can be abstracted for machine learning.

Learn More

Interdisciplinary Collaboration

Actively collaborating with psychologists, computer scientists, and AI researchers to address complex questions about human language and thought. Emphasizes cross-disciplinary approaches to innovation.
