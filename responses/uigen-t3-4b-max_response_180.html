Key Properties of Black Holes

Mass of the Black Hole

The mass of a black hole determines its gravitational pull and its event horizon size.

10 Solar Masses

A solar mass is approximately 1.989 × 10^30 kg.

Spin (Angular Momentum)

The spin of a black hole affects the shape of its event horizon and the surrounding accretion disk.

0.0 (Non-rotating)

A value of 1 means it's spinning at its maximum possible rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape its pull. It forms when a massive star collapses under its own gravity.

The more mass a black hole has, the stronger its gravitational pull, and the larger its event horizon. A more massive black hole can also have more significant tidal forces.

The event horizon is the boundary around a black hole beyond which nothing, not even light, can escape. It's the "point of no return" for anything that gets too close.
